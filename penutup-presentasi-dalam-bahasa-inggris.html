Discussing about the advantages of sorting the trash brings us to the end of my presentation. I welcome of any suggestions, corrections, inputs, and questions relating to my topic. I deliver my best gratitude for your fully attention during my presentation. Thank you, Good Afternoon.

Pembahasan:

Membahas tentang keuntungan-keuntungan memilah sampah membawa kita pada akhir presentasi saya. Saya terbuka untuk segala saran, koreksi, masukkan, dan pertanyaan yang berhubungan dengan topic saya. 14. buatin kata penutup untuk presentasi bahasa inggris dong


Kelas : SMA
Pelajaran : Bahasa Inggris
Kategori : Presentation
Kata kunci : closing presentation

Pembahasan:
Ada tiga tahapan dalam menutup presentasi:
1. Summarize they key points and close the presentation.
2. Thank the audience
3. Take questions
4. Provide next steps or contact information

Contoh closing presentation:
It’s time to bring this presentation to a close.  I’d like to summarize by saying ... 
And that brings us to the end. I’d like to thank you for your time and attention today.
I would now be interested to hear from you with your thoughts or questions.
If anyone who like more information or has questions, please feel free to contact me at ....

contoh closing presentation about five days school:

The Purpose of This Presentation Is To Know What Is Telling The Time.

There are two common ways of telling the time:

1) Say the hour first and then the minutes. (Hour + Minutes)

6:25 - It's six twenty-five8:05 - It's eight O-five (the O is said like the letter O)9:11 - It's nine eleven2:34 - It's two thirty-four

2) Say the minutes first and then the hour. (Minutes + PAST / TO + Hour)

For minutes 1-30 we use PAST after the minutes.

For minutes 31-59 we use TO after the minutes.

2:35 - It's twenty-five to three11:20 - It's twenty past eleven4:18 - It's eighteen past four8:51 - It's nine to nine2:59 - It's one to three

When it is 15 minutes past the hour we normally say: (a) quarter past

7:15 - It's (a) quarter past seven

When it is 15 minutes before the hour we normally say: a quarter to

12:45 - It's (a) quarter to one

When it is 30 minutes past the hour we normally say: half past

3:30 - It's half past three (but we can also say three-thirty)

O'clock

We use o'clock when there are NO minutes.

10:00 - It's ten o'clock

5:00 - It's five o'clock

1:00 - It's one o'clock

Sometimes it is written as 9 o'clock (the number + o'clock)

12:00

For 12:00 there are four expressions in English.

twelve o'clock midday = noon midnight

Asking for the Time

The common question forms we use to ask for the time right now are:

What time is it?What is the time?

A more polite way to ask for the time, especially from a stranger is:

Could you tell me the time please?

The common question forms we use to ask at what time a specific event will happen are:

What time...?

When...?

What time does the flight to New York leave?When does the bus arrive from London?When does the concert begin?

Giving The Time

We use It is or It's to respond to the questions that ask for the time right now.

It is half past five (5:30).It's ten to twelve (11:50)

We use the structure AT + time when giving the time of a specific event.

The bus arrives at midday (12:00).The flight leaves at a quarter to two (1:45).The concert begins at ten o'clock. (10:00)

We can also use subject pronouns in these responses.

It arrives at midday (12:00).It leaves at a quarter to two (1:45).It begins at ten o'clock. (10:00)

AM vs. PM

We don't normally use the 24-hour clock in English.

We use a.m. (am) for the morning and p.m. (pm) for the afternoon and night.

3am = Three o'clock in the morning.

3pm = Three o'clock in the afternoon.
