9. Buatlah teks deskriptentasi berbahasa inggris (3 paragraf) tentang nasi gandul mengenai makanan/kue tradisional yang sudah mulai langka di daerah kalian!

Gandul Rice

Nasi Gandul or Sego Gandul (Javanese: Sega gandhul) is a special dish originating from the Pati area, Central Java, Indonesia which at first glance is similar to Beef Stew and Goulash. Nasi gandul at first glance is similar to a combination of soto and gule, in the form of meat with a brownish sauce with a sweet savory taste.

Nasi Gandul is a specialty of the Pati region (coastal area of Central Java, north coast road of Java). The area in Pati that popularized this gandul rice is Gajahmati Village (south of the Pati bus terminal. Semampir Village/East of Gajahmati Village), which is why the words Nasi Gandul Gajah Mati are often encountered. Although in the end there were many sellers of nasi gandul who did not come from Gajahmati Village, they still wrote the word Gajahmati Village on the banner where they ate. If we trace the origin of the name Nasi Gandul, there are many versions that say about it.

The Nasi Gandul stall that has been around for a long time and is trusted by the people of Pati is Pak Meled's Warung Nasi Gandul. Meled admitted that his Nasi Gandul stall was originally run by his parents since 1955. It started with selling around the village until it was finally passed on to him and he had a permanent place to sell.

19. buatlah teks deskripsi berbahasa INGGRIS (3 paragraf) mengenai makanan/kue tradisional yang sudah mulai langka di daerah kalian!


                                                     Klepon

Klepon is one of Indonesia traditional foods. Klepon is a green-colored ball of rice cake filled with liquid palm sugar and coated in grated coconut. Why the color is green? It is because the dough is flavored and colored with pandan or suji leaves. 
The origin of Klepon is from Central Java. In other parts of Indonesia, such as Sulawesi and Sumatra, Klepon is known as Onde-onde. The dish is also called Klepon in Netherlands. Klepon was introduced by Indonesia immigrants to the Netherlands in 1950.
Klepon traditionally served in a banana leaf but nowadays, Klepon is packed in plastic wrappings. One must be careful on consuming Klepon. Because when you bite Klepon, the bite could squirted and the liquid palm sugar might stained your shirt.
