3. what is a explanation and discussion text


Explanation text is a statement about how or why something is the way it is. If you are failing math, you should better come up with a good explanation to give to your family or your partner.

Discussion text is defined as a text to present a problematic discourse only. A discussion text is commonly found in philosophical, historic, and social writing.

4. what is the generic struktur of discussion text


Generic structure of discussion text:

1. Issue or Problem

Pada struktur ini, terdapat masalah yang disajikan penulis untuk ditemukan jalan tengahnya di struktur akhir. Biasanya, masalah pada discussion text yakni isu yang sedang ramai diperbincangkan.

2. Argument

Argument pro

Atau argumen pro, maksudnya adalah pendapat dari satu sudut pandang yang maknanya mendukung isu atau masalah tersebut. Namun, tentunya pro argument akan disangkal oleh pihak lain yakni argument cons.

Argument cons

Atau argumen kontra, maksudnya adalah pendapat dari satu sudut pandang yang maknanya menyangkal atau melawan isu atau masalah tersebut.

3. Recommendation

Berisi rekomendasi penyelesaian masalah-masalah tersebut. Penyelesaian masalah tidak dapat berpihak pada salah satu sudut pandang, maka dari itu harus diambil dari jalan tengahnya.

16. Contoh discussion text what should do after school


Giving Children Homework

There are a lot of discussion as to whether children should be given homework or not. Is it enough for children having time to study at school or needing additional time in home for study after school time?

Some people claim that children do enough work in school already. They also argue that children have their hobbies which they want to do after school, such as sport or music. A further point they make is that a lot of homeworks are pointless and does not help the children learn at all.

However, there are also strong arguments against this point of view. Parents and teachers argue that it is important to find out whether children can work on their own without the support from the teacher. They say that the evening is a good time for children to sit down and think about what they have learned in school.

Furthermore they claim that the school day is too short to get anything done. It makes sense to send home tasks like independent reading or further writing task which do not need the teacher support.

I think, on balance, that some homework is good idea but that should only given at the weekend when children have more time.

17. what is the purpose, generic structure, language featurers of discussion text.......


General structure of Discussion Text

– Issue : stating the issue which is to discussed
– Argument pro : presenting the point in in supporting the presented issue
– Argument cons : presenting other points which disagree to the supporting point
– Conclusion / recommendation : stating the writer’ recommendation of the discourse

Purpose of Discussion text

– To present two points of view about issue or problem.
– To present arguments from differing points of view about issue or problem..



Language feature of Discussion text

– Introducing category or generic participant
– Using thinking verb; feel, hope, believe, etc
– Using contrastive conjunction; however, on the other hand, but, in other side, although, etc
– Using modalities; must, should, could, may, etc
– Using adverbial of manner; deliberately, hopefully, etc
– Simple present

26. perasamaan explanation what is simililarity of discussion text and explanation text


Discussion text and explanation text both will tell the reader facts surrounding a specific topic. However, discussion text has components of several points of few and has gaps for the reader to come up with a conclusion. An explanation text is more straightforward, only stating facts without contradicting points.

28. what is the purpose and the general structure of discussion text


Purpose:
To present information and opinions about more than one side of an issue("for" points and "against" points").

Generic structure:
•Issue Opening statement presenting the issue
•Arguments pro or evidence for different points of view (pros and cons)
•Arguments against
•Conclusion or recommendation
