The process of photosynthesis can be described in the following steps:

Carbon dioxide in the air enters the leaves of plants through the stomata.

Water enters the leaves, mainly through plant roots. The water enters the plant roots and then the water is channeled to the leaves through the stem.

When sunlight falls on the leaf surface, chlorophyll captures the energy from the light. Chlorophyll is a green pigment that can be found in plant leaves. Energy is used to convert water into hydrogen and oxygen. Hydrogen is combined with carbon dioxide to produce food for the plant, while oxygen is released by the plant through the stomata.

The formula for the whole process of photosynthesis can be written as follows:

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Sunlight ----> C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

CO₂ = carbon dioxide

H₂O = water

C₆H₁₂O₆ = glucose

O₂ = oxygen

Photosynthesis is also useful for humans and animals because this process helps maintain the oxygen and carbon dioxide level in the air. Almost all the oxygen in the air comes from this process. The chemical energy stored by plants is transferred to animals and humans when plants are consumed.
