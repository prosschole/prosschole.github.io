9. Elaborate the distinetion between formal and informal invitation letter !


Kelas: SMP
Pelajaran: English
Kategori: writing a letter
Pembahasan:

There are several distinction or difference between formal and informal letter.
- at the beginning of the formal letter, one states the clear intention of the letter such as: I am writing to complain about ...../to inquire ...../ to apply for the post....
In informal letter, one says the greetings and states the general condition of him/her plus asks how they are doing at the beginning.
- in the content, formal letter does not include phrasal verbs, the abbreviation/ shortened version of certain words like ASAP or y'all and uses the formal words inteade.
In informal letter, such phrases are commonly used.
- in the ending, formal letter uses expression such as 'I look forward to hearing from you'. Meanwhile, in informal one, such is not always used.

Kelas: SMA
Pelajaran: English
Kategori: formal invitation
Pembahasan:

1. Expression used in formal invitation usually has formal, uncommon words with longer sentences. Example:
  In informal invitation for a party, you'd just say, 'Come to my birthday party, dudes. There'll be free loads of drink and food.'
 In formal one, you'd have to say. 'You are cordially invited to the ball (instead of using the word 'party') of Sir Arthur Mason at the Coulton Mansion. Special refreshments (instead of food and drinks) are available.'
2. The difference between formal and informal letters are
  - the beginning:
    formal letters starts with the reason why you write the letter . e.g:
    'I am writing to inquire about the information on....'  
    Informal letters starts with greetings and asking about how is the receiver.
   - the diction / choice of words, in which the formal and uncommon ones are used in the formal one and likewise in informal one.
3. part of formal one that shows the difference is
    - the usage of complete written form in sentence, no shortened form such as I'm, she's, they'll. Instead, the ' I am, She is and They will' are used.
    - the absence of phrasal verbs in it such as: go out, get down etc.
    - the usage of frequent passive voice in the formal one.
4. The RSVP in the invitation, if stated, means that the one who are invited are expected to reply and inform that he/she is confirmed to be able to come. So, such is the function.

16. mention the differences between formal invitation and informal invitation!​


Jawaban:

The main difference between formal and informal letters is that formal letters professionally address someone, and informal letters address someone in a personal way. Other differences include:

Formal letters follow a specific format, while informal letters can follow any format.

The tone of a formal letter is professional and official, while the tone of an informal letter is friendly.

People usually write formal letters in the first or third person, while informal letters can be from any point of view.

Formal letters are focused and concise and do not include filler or fluff.

Formal letters are usually typed, while informal letters can also be handwritten.

Formal letters include a date and contact information at the top.

Unlike informal letters, formal letters do not include grammatical trends like contractions (can't, don't, I've), slang or idioms.

Most formal letters are no longer than one page, while informal letters can be any length.

When trying to decide what type of letter to write, choose a formal one when communicating with an official or someone you only know professionally. Choose an informal letter when writing casually to someone you know very well, such as a co-worker.
