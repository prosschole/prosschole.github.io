The orange is a usually round or oval citrus fruit with an orange rind and flesh. It grows on a tree that can reach 10 meters high, has dark green shiny leaves and small white flowers. The fruit is a modified berry known as a hesperidium, and the flesh is divided into segments called carpels. Oranges are consumed fresh or in juice, used to make stewed fruit, jams, for consumption as dehydrated fruit, etc. A whole orange contains on average only 85 calories and has no fat, cholesterol or sodium.
