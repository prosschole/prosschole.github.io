2. Generally, there are three structures in an explanation text. It begins with an introduction then moves to a sequence of explanation and ends it with a closure.

3. A sequence of explanation is a part where the writer tries to inform the reader how the phenomenon or event may take place or happen. It usually begins by starting from the very first part of a phenomenon until the phenomenon is clearly explained and reaches the conclusion. The length of the explanation might depend on how complex the event or the phenomenon is.
