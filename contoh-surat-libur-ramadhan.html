Jawaban:

Last year during Ramadan, I spent my holiday at home with my family. Even though we couldn't go anywhere due to the pandemic, we still had a great time together.

During the day, we usually spent our time preparing for iftar, the meal that we have after sunset during Ramadan. My mother and I would cook various dishes, such as fried rice, chicken curry, and beef rendang. We also made some snacks, like banana fritters and spring rolls.

In the afternoon, we would take a nap or read the Quran. Sometimes, we also watched Islamic movies or TV shows that were airing during Ramadan.

When it was time for iftar, we would gather around the table and break our fast together. We usually had some dates, followed by a soup or salad, and then the main dishes. The food was always delicious and it was great to have a variety of dishes every day.

After iftar, we would usually spend some time praying and then we would watch TV or play games together. Sometimes, we also went out for a walk around the neighborhood to enjoy the cool night breeze.

Overall, even though we couldn't travel anywhere during Ramadan due to the pandemic, we still had a wonderful time at home with our family. It was a peaceful and meaningful holiday spent in the company of our loved ones.

Recount Text using Simple Past Tense

My Ramadan Holiday

Last year during the month of ramadan, I had the opportunity to take a week-long holiday to celebrate this special month with my family. Ramadan is a holy month for muslims, during which we fast from dawn until sunset and focus on prayer, charity, and reflection.

On the first day of the holiday we woke up early to have our pre-dawn meal, called suhoor. after praying the morning prayer, we begin our day of fasting. It was difficult at first, but we soon adjusted to the routine. We spend most of the day at home, reading the Quran and doing good deeds. As the sun began to set we eagerly waited the call to prayer, signaling the end of the fast. We broke our fast with dates and water, as is tradition, and then had a delicious meal with our family. we talked about our day and how we could improve our fasting experiences in the coming days.

Throughout the week we continue this routine, breaking our fast together as a family and spending time in prayer and reflection. We also took the opportunity to visit our local mosque and attend nightly prayers, which are held only during ramadan.

On the last day of our holiday, we celebrated Eid al-Fitr, which marks the end of Ramadan. We woke up early to perform the Eid prayer and then visited friends and family to exchange gifts and share festive meals.

Overall, my Ramadan holiday was a memorable experience. It was a time to focus on spiritual growth and strengthen family bonds. I look forward to celebrating Ramadan again this year with the same enthusiasm and dedication.

28. contoh surat tentang liburan dalam bahasa inggris beserta artinya (surat nya yang agak panjang)


Dear Regina,

How are you? I hope you’re fine. I’m writing this letter to tell you that I really miss you. Do you miss me too? It’s too bad that we couldn’t spend the last holiday together. I spent my holiday in Malang. I visited some places in the city, but among those places, Mount Bromo left the greatest impression on me.

Let me tell you how my tour in Mount Bromo went. I spent about 3 hours to get to Mount Bromo from Malang by public transportations. I arrived at the location in the afternoon. Since I wanted to watch the sunrise from the top of Penanjakan Hill which was one of the best sunrise spot in Mount Bromo, I decided to take a rest at a guest house and would go to the hill at the dawn. I woke up at 3 in the morning and as soon as I was ready, I rent a jeep to get to Penanjakan Hill. When arriving at the hill, many people were there. Long story short, I was finally at the top of the hill and witnessed the remarkable view of the golden sunrise. I spent around 1 hour there just enjoying the breathtaking view which the nature offered.

After that, I went to Savannah Hill also known as the Telletubbies Hill of Bromo by the same jeep. Again, the view was just so green and bizarre. It felt peaceful in there. Then, the jeep took me to the Whispering Sands. It may have been only the sea of sands, but the atmosphere there was so surreal. I felt like I was visiting the different planet. The next destination was the Bromo Crater itself. The jeep dropped me off at the jeep parking area around 1 to 2 km from the crater. I could either walk or ride a horse to get to the crater. It only took less than 30 minutes to the top. On the top of the mountain, it was not the crater that stole my attention, but the scenery. Everyone and everything looked so tiny from there. Love,
